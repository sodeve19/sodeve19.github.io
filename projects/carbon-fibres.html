Carbon Fibres for Brain-Machine Interfaces

Neural Engineering Cortical Implants Seizure Prediction

Overview

This project focuses on the development and characterisation of carbon fibre electrodes for cortical recording and seizure prediction, in collaboration with Carbon Cybernetics and RMIT University. Carbon fibres offer a unique combination of mechanical compliance, small diameter, and electrochemical stability that makes them ideal candidates for long-term neural interfaces.

Motivation

Conventional metal electrodes suffer from a chronic foreign-body response that degrades recording quality over time. Carbon fibres, with their tissue-like stiffness and sub-micron diameters, minimise mechanical mismatch and inflammatory scarring, enabling stable, high-fidelity cortical recordings suitable for closed-loop seizure detection and intervention systems.

Approach

  • Fabrication and surface functionalisation of individual carbon fibre electrodes.
  • Electrochemical characterisation (EIS, CV) to assess electrode impedance and charge-injection capacity.
  • In vitro and in vivo validation of recording performance and tissue response.
  • Integration with signal processing pipelines for seizure prediction algorithms.
