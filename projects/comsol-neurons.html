Simulating Neurons and Their Interactions with Electrodes in COMSOL

COMSOL FEM Simulation Neural Modelling

Overview

This project uses finite element modelling (FEM) in COMSOL Multiphysics to simulate neuronal behaviour and the complex interactions at the electrode–tissue interface. The models inform the design and optimisation of neural recording and stimulation devices, reducing the need for costly and time-intensive in vivo experiments during early development stages.

Motivation

Understanding how electric fields propagate through neural tissue and how electrodes couple to individual neurons is critical for designing effective neural interfaces. Computational models allow rapid parameter sweeps — varying electrode geometry, material properties, and placement — to predict recording sensitivity and stimulation selectivity before committing to fabrication.

Approach

  • Construction of anatomically-informed 3D tissue geometries incorporating cortical layers and electrode geometries.
  • Coupling electrostatic/AC electric field models with Hodgkin–Huxley neuron models.
  • Parameter studies on electrode diameter, material conductivity, and insertion depth.
  • Validation of simulation outputs against experimental EIS and recording data.

Status

Active modelling and validation against experimental results.
