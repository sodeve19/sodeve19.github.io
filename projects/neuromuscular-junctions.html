Neuromuscular Junctions In Vitro

In Vitro Electrophysiology Neuroscience

Overview

This project studies the formation and electrophysiology of neuromuscular junctions (NMJs) using in vitro models. By co-culturing motor neurons and skeletal muscle cells on multi-electrode arrays, we capture the electrical activity of developing NMJs to advance understanding of motor neuron diseases and facilitate drug screening applications.

Motivation

Neuromuscular junction dysfunction underlies devastating diseases such as ALS and myasthenia gravis. Reliable in vitro models that recapitulate NMJ electrophysiology provide a tractable platform for studying disease mechanisms, testing therapeutic compounds, and reducing reliance on animal models in early-stage drug discovery.

Approach

  • Co-culture of iPSC-derived motor neurons and myotubes on multi-electrode arrays (MEAs).
  • Electrophysiological characterisation of spontaneous and evoked NMJ activity.
  • Immunofluorescence imaging to confirm NMJ formation and morphology.
  • Pharmacological perturbation studies to validate the model's drug-response fidelity.

Status

Active in vitro experiments and electrophysiological data collection.
