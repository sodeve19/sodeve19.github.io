Smart-Bra: Impedance-Based Breast Cancer Monitoring

Wearable Devices Bioimpedance Oncology

Overview

The Smart-Bra is a wearable device that uses bioelectrical impedance analysis (BIA) for continuous, non-invasive monitoring of breast tissue changes associated with cancer progression. By embedding textile electrodes directly into a garment, the system enables longitudinal tracking without the discomfort or cost of clinical imaging.

Motivation

Breast cancer detection typically relies on periodic mammography, which may miss rapid changes between screenings. A wearable continuous-monitoring solution could flag impedance anomalies indicative of tumour growth or inflammatory changes, enabling earlier clinical intervention and reducing patient anxiety through passive, at-home use.

Approach

  • Design and integration of flexible textile electrodes into a wearable garment form factor.
  • Multi-frequency bioelectrical impedance measurement circuitry for tissue characterisation.
  • Signal processing and machine learning models to classify tissue impedance signatures.
  • Clinical validation studies comparing impedance maps against standard imaging modalities.

Status

Prototype development and benchtop validation.
